# Construction AI Billing — Marketing Channel Plan
## "Go Live" Launch Playbook

---

## Phase: Soft Launch (now, pre-Product Hunt)

### 1. Reddit — Organic Community Posts

**Target subreddits (in order of priority):**
- r/Construction — 280K members, broad GC + owner audience
- r/Contractors — 90K members, highest intent (actual GCs)
- r/generalcontractor — smaller but hyper-targeted
- r/smallbusiness — good for "I built a tool" posts

**Post angle:**
> *"I'm a GC who got fed up with Excel for pay apps. So I built a free web tool that generates AIA G702/G703 PDFs from your Schedule of Values in 60 seconds. No signup walls to try it. Would love feedback from anyone who does progress billing."*

**Rules:**
- Post from a personal account that has history in the sub (never a brand-new account)
- Engage in the comments — answer every question
- Don't post the same content in multiple subs on the same day (spread 3–5 days apart)
- Screenshot the G702 PDF in the post image — show don't just tell

**Key message:** Free. Takes 60 seconds. No credit card. Built by a GC.

---

### 2. LinkedIn — Personal Post from Vagish Kapila

**Target audience:** GC owners, project managers, construction accountants, field supers

**Post format:**
> Short story (5–7 sentences) → problem → what you built → screenshot → CTA

**Draft:**
> Every month I watched our team spend 3 hours doing AIA pay apps in Excel.
> Formulas breaking. Wrong retainage. PDF formatting nightmares.
> So I built a tool that does it in 60 seconds. You upload your Schedule of Values, fill in this period's work, and it generates a compliant AIA G702/G703 PDF — retainage calculated automatically, lien waivers included.
> It's live and free to try: constructinv.varshyl.com
> Would love feedback from anyone who does progress billing. What's the most painful part of your billing workflow?

**Image:** Side-by-side of a messy Excel sheet → clean G702 PDF output

**Timing:** Post Tuesday or Wednesday morning, 7–9 AM local time (highest engagement)

**Hashtags:** #ConstructionManagement #GeneralContractor #ConstructionTech #AIA #ProjectManagement

---

### 3. Facebook Groups

**Target groups (private/closed — requires manual join):**
- "General Contractors Network" — 45K+ members
- "Construction Business Owners" — 30K+ members
- "AEC Tech" — 15K+ members (Architecture, Engineering, Construction)
- "Construction Contractors & Builders" — various regional groups

**Approach:**
- Join 3–5 groups as Vagish (personal account)
- Wait 48 hours after joining before posting (avoid spam filters)
- Post in "question" format, not a product pitch:
  > *"Fellow GCs — what tool are you using for AIA pay apps? We built our own (free) and trying to understand if others have the same pain points we did."*
- Respond in comments linking to the tool if people express interest

**Avoid:** Nextdoor — confirmed residential/homeowner audience, not commercial GCs

---

### 4. Product Hunt — Planned for later

**When:** After the first 50–100 real users are using it (social proof)

**Prep checklist before launch:**
- [ ] Maker profile set up on producthunt.com
- [ ] Gallery images ready (5 screenshots): landing page, new project wizard, G702 PDF, lien waiver modal, settings
- [ ] Short demo video (60–90 seconds) — screen recording of full workflow
- [ ] "Tagline" locked: *"AIA G702/G703 pay apps in 60 seconds — built for field teams"*
- [ ] Have 10 early users ready to upvote on launch day
- [ ] Launch on a Tuesday or Wednesday (best days for PH)
- [ ] Be online all day to respond to comments

**Positioning on PH:**
> Category: Productivity / SaaS
> Not positioning as "AI" (oversaturated) — positioning as "construction billing automation"

## Phase: Growth (post-launch)

### 5. Construction Trade Associations
- AGC (Associated General Contractors) — local chapter newsletters
- CFMA (Construction Financial Management Association) — their members are exactly the target user (controllers, CFOs, PMs)
- ASA (American Subcontractors Association) — subcontractors need lien waivers too

**Approach:** Reach out to chapter communications directors with a 2–3 sentence pitch + offer for a member-exclusive free trial.

### 6. YouTube / Loom Demos
- 90-second "How it works" video → upload to YouTube → embed on landing page
- Loom "walkthroughs" for specific workflows (e.g., "How to generate a CA lien waiver in 30 seconds")

### 7. SEO Content
- Blog posts targeting: "AIA G702 template free", "how to fill out AIA G703", "construction lien waiver California", "AIA pay application software"
- Long-tail: "how to calculate retainage on a pay application"

## Metrics to Track

- Weekly signups (target: 10/week in first month)
- Projects created per user (target: >1 means they came back)
- PDF downloads per project (proves the core feature is used)
- Reddit/LinkedIn referral traffic (UTM params: `?utm_source=reddit&utm_medium=post`)
